Why Ride?

{%endblock%} {% block content %}

Benefits

  • Learning to take care of a horse (grooming, tacking up, cleaning up) teaches responsibility
  • Teaches sportsmanship
  • You learn to trust a horse, and you learn how to make the horse trust you
  • Riding builds core strength and balance
  • Jumping feels like flying and who doesn't want to fly?
  • You are able to forget all your worries and just focus on riding

Finding the Right Barn

  • Tour the barn first: see how the horses are treated, is the barn well maintained?
  • Meet the trainers: do you feel respected? Do you think this trainer will be able to get you to the level you want?
  • Think about where you want to end up: do you want to be competing at a high level eventually? Is the barn going to allow that?

What Apparel Do You Need for Hunt Seat Style Riding? (and how much will it cost you*)

  • In the beginning
    • Helmet ($30-$600): A cheaper helmet (not a bike helmet) will be fine in the beginning, but once you want a nice show helmet you can get one for around $200-$300
    • Heeled Boots: any cheap 1/2 inch heeled boots will be fine in the beginning, but eventually you will want paddock boots (approx: $100) and half chaps (approx: $50-$150)
    • Knee Patch Breeches ($30-$300): you will be fine with cheaper breeches in the beginning, but nicer show breeches can get expensive
  • Showing Apparel (on top of preliminary equipment)
    • Show Helmet: see above
    • Show Breeches: see above; tan color
    • Field Boots ($200-$1000+): get black leather
    • Show Shirt ($50-$200): get white long sleeve shirt
    • Show Coat ($200-$1000+)
    • Hairnet (approx: $8)
    • Boot Socks (approx: $12 per pair)
    • Belt ($30-$60): get leather to match boots
    • Gloves ($20-$80): get black leather
  • *Keep in mind these are just rough estimates, and you can often find good deals on sites such as Dover Saddlery and used apparel on Ebay
