Why Ride?

Benefits

  • Learning to take care of a horse (grooming, tacking up, cleaning up) teaches responsibility
  • Teaches sportsmanship
  • You learn to trust a horse, and you learn how to make the horse trust you
  • Riding helps balance in everyday life
  • Jumping feels like flying: who doesn't want to fly?
  • You are able to forget all your other worries and just focus on riding

Finding the Right Barn

  • Tour the barn first: see how the horses are treated, is the barn well maintained?
  • Meet the trainers: do you feel respected? Do you think this trainer will be able to get you to the level you want to be at?
  • Think about where you want to end up: do you want to be competing at a high level eventually? Is the barn going to allow that?

What Apparel Do You Need for Hunt Seat Style Riding? (and how much will it cost you)

  • In the beginning
    • Helmet ($30-$600, $30 will be fine in the beginning, but once you want a nice show helmet you can get one for around $200-$300)
    • Heeled Boots (any cheap heeled boots will be fine in the beginning, but eventually you will want paddock boots (approx: $100) and half chaps (approx: $50-$150)
    • Knee Patch Breeches ($30-$300, you will be fine with cheaper breeches in the begining, but nicer show breeches can get expensive)
  • Showing Apparel (on top of)
    • Show Helmet: see above
    • Show Breeches: see above; tan color
    • Field Boots ($200-$1000+ for black leather)
    • Show Shirt ($50-$200, get white long sleeve shirt)
    • Show Coat ($200-$1000+)
    • Hairnet (approx: $8)
    • Boot Socks (approx: $12 per pair)
    • Belt ($30-$60 for leather)
    • Gloves ($20-$80, get black leather)

    *Keep in mind these are just rough estimates, and you can often find good deals on sites such as Dover Saddlery and used apparel on Ebay
