About Me

Welcome to my webpage! As you might be able to tell, I am an avid horse lover. As a result, I have been taking riding lessons since the age of seven, and competing since the 9th grade. I have taken lessons at many barns throughout the metro detroit area, and have trained in a few of the many different riding disciplines. As of now, I spend the school year riding hunter/jumper style with the Michigan Equestrian Team at Willowbrooke Farm, and breaks and summers at Windermere Equestrian Center.

Places I've Ridden

When I was seven, I went horseback riding with my Girl Scout troop at Camp Innisfree (which has since closed). I thoroughly enjoyed myself, so my mom signed me up to take lessons. I spent about a year taking weekly lessons at the camp before I decided that I wasn't advancing to the level I wanted to be. It was then that I moved to Brilliant Reflection Farm, where I took bi-weekly dressage lessons. I loved the barn, my trainer, and the horses I rode there (Alex and Thunder), but I dreamed of learning how to jump, which is not a part of dressage. I took a year off of riding to pursue figure skating, but then decided I was ready to get back in the saddle. I moved to Big Lake Farm, where a family friend was riding. Long story short, I wanted be at a barn with more emphasis on competion and a jumping mindset, which Big Lake just didn't offer. When I was in the 7th grade I made a new friend at school that also rode and jumped horses, and I was very interested to find out where she rode. She told me that she took lessons at Windermere Equestrian Center, and my mom made an appointment for an evaluation lesson. When I first got to Windermere I was amazed by how many horses there were, and how large the lesson program was. When I rode, the owner, Miss Jen, said that I had lots to learn, and that she wanted me to get into a weekly lesson group. I was sold on the place, and still call Windermere home to this day. It is where I became the rider I am today. I learned to jump and problem solve on a variety of different horses. I love Windermere, and even though I would say I'm very confident on horseback, I know I still have much to learn.
